# Business Sales Performance Analytics 📊

## Overview
This project analyzes real-world business sales data to uncover revenue trends, top-performing products, and high-value regions. The goal is to provide actionable insights that help businesses make data-driven decisions related to growth, inventory planning, and market focus.

This project was completed as part of **Data Science & Analytics – Task 1 (2026)** under the **Future Interns Internship Program**.

---

## Problem Statement
Businesses often have large volumes of sales data but lack clarity on:
- Which products generate the most revenue
- How sales performance changes over time
- Which regions or markets contribute the highest value
- Where to focus efforts to maximize growth

This project addresses these questions using sales data analysis and interactive dashboards.

---

## Dataset
- **Online Retail Dataset (Kaggle)**
- Real-world e-commerce transaction data
- Includes invoices, products, quantities, prices, customers, and countries

---

## Tools & Technologies
- **Power BI** – Data cleaning, modeling, DAX, and dashboard creation
- **Excel / CSV** – Raw data handling
- **DAX** – KPI and measure calculations

---

## Key KPIs Analyzed
- **Total Revenue**
- **Total Orders**
- **Average Order Value (AOV)**
- **Top 10 Products by Revenue**
- **Revenue by Country**
- **Revenue Trends Over Time**

---

## Dashboard Features
- Interactive KPI cards for quick performance overview
- Time-series analysis of revenue trends
- Top 10 products contributing to overall revenue
- Country-wise revenue distribution
- Filters for time period and valid sales

---

## Key Insights
- A small group of products contributes a significant portion of total revenue.
- Sales show noticeable seasonality with clear peak periods.
- The United Kingdom dominates total revenue, while some other regions show higher average order values.
- Returns and invalid transactions can significantly impact revenue metrics if not handled correctly.

---

## Business Recommendations
- Focus inventory and marketing efforts on top-performing products.
- Plan promotions and campaigns around high-revenue periods.
- Explore expansion or targeted strategies in high-AOV regions.
- Monitor returns closely to protect overall revenue performance.
